The room holds the shape of having been in it —
chair pulled slightly out, book facedown on the table,
a glass of water that will not be finished.

Light moves without apology.
It crosses the floor, finds the photograph,
turns it into a small, bright animal.

You are not here, and yet
the room insists you were,
and will be again, in the same posture,
reading the same page.

I close the door. The light continues.
