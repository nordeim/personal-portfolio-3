---
title: A Storm of Fire and Ice
description: A narrative poem inspired by the fire at Miss Maudie's house in To Kill a Mockingbird.
status: Literature response
---

A night to remember
For many Decembers
The coldest of moons
All huddling cocoons

The clock struck one
Awake were none
Next thing I knew
The world was askew

The Maudie's ablaze
County a haze
An orangey blur
That gave me the shudder

All lent a hand
Throwing buckets of sand
None tried to flee
Not even old N. Radley

A red truck came
But fire not tamed
Some furniture survived
Then two more arrived

With effort combined
All was made fine
None were injured
'Cept Avery bit hurt

A surprise it was
That Maudie felt no loss
Must've been the flue said she
But is it true? Maybe.

Breaking news: Even Boo Radley
Seems to have been there briefly!
